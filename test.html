1) Executive Summary

A clear narrative: what you do, for whom, why now, and what you’re asking for.

Northwind Studio Co. is a boutique product design & build studio helping early-stage teams ship high-quality marketing sites and lightweight web apps in weeks—not quarters. We focus on conversion-driven UX, crisp visual identity, and performant front-end engineering.

Problem: founders and lean marketing teams struggle to deliver premium web experiences quickly; freelancers are inconsistent, and agencies are expensive and slow.

Solution: standardized delivery “tracks” (Brand Sprint → Web Launch → Growth Iterations) with transparent pricing and a repeatable process.

Business Snapshot

Services + Retainers

Project fees fund momentum; retainers stabilize cash flow.

Funding (Sample)

$120k

Optional seed to hire 1 engineer + 1 growth lead, extend runway, and increase throughput.

2) Company Overview

Mission, differentiation, and the operating model.

Mission: make world-class web experiences accessible to small teams.

Vision: become the go-to studio for “launch quality” digital products in the $10k–$60k range.

Business model:

  • Fixed-scope packages for predictable delivery (launches, redesigns, brand refresh).
  • Monthly retainers for iteration (A/B testing, landing pages, performance, content updates).
  • Partner referrals with small consultancies and no-code builders (rev-share).

Competitive advantage: brand-level aesthetics with engineering-grade execution; small-team speed; documented process; and strong conversion focus (copy + layout + performance).

3) Market & Customers

Define the ICP and why they buy. Be specific.

Ideal customer profile (ICP):

  • Seed–Series A startups needing a credible web presence and a clear conversion funnel.
  • Professional services firms (legal, accounting, consulting) modernizing brand + lead gen.
  • Creator-led products launching paid offerings.

Customer triggers: fundraising, product launch, rebrand, poor conversion, slow site performance, inconsistent messaging.

Market size (directional): large and fragmented. The target is not “all businesses,” but teams with budgets for quality and a need for speed.

4) Services & Pricing

Packages reduce friction; retainers improve stability.

Core packages (sample):

  • Brand Sprint (1–2 weeks): positioning, visual direction, landing page wireframes. $6k–$12k
  • Web Launch (3–5 weeks): full marketing site, design system, SEO basics, analytics. $18k–$45k
  • Growth Iterations (monthly): 2–4 experiments, landing pages, content pipeline. $4k–$12k/mo

Positioning

Premium, not bloated

Agency quality with a small, senior team and a repeatable playbook.

Margins

60–75%

High gross margin services; costs scale with headcount and contractors.

5) Go-to-Market (GTM)

A few strong channels beat many weak ones.

Primary channels:

  • Content + case studies: teardown posts, before/after performance metrics, conversion wins.
  • Founder-led outbound: targeted lists, personalized loom audits, clear offer and CTA.
  • Partnerships: fractional CMOs, brand strategists, and boutique dev shops.

Sales motion: 20-minute discovery → paid audit (optional) → scoped proposal → kickoff deposit.

KPIs: lead-to-call rate, call-to-proposal, proposal-to-close, average project value, retainer attach rate, delivery cycle time.

6) Operations

How work is delivered, quality is ensured, and scope is controlled.

Delivery process (sample):

  • Week 0: kickoff, goals, analytics baseline, content inventory
  • Week 1: sitemap + wireframes + messaging outline
  • Week 2: visual design + component system
  • Week 3–4: build, QA, SEO + performance pass
  • Week 5: launch + post-launch measurement

Tooling: Figma, linear issue tracking, automated performance checks, accessibility review, and deployment previews.

Quality bar: responsive by default, Lighthouse targets, semantic HTML, accessible components, documented handoff.

7) Team

Keep it believable: roles, responsibilities, and hiring plan.

Founding team (sample):

  • Creative Director: brand, UX, visual system, client strategy
  • Lead Engineer: front-end architecture, performance, implementation

12-month hiring plan (optional): contract copywriter (month 2), part-time PM (month 4), full-time engineer (month 6–9).

9) Risks & Mitigation

Show you’ve thought through what could break—and how you’ll respond.

  • Pipeline volatility: mitigate with retainers, partnerships, and consistent publishing cadence.
  • Scope creep: mitigate with clear SOWs, change orders, and weekly checkpoints.
  • Delivery bottlenecks: mitigate with templates, component libraries, and selective subcontracting.
  • Market downturn: mitigate by targeting recession-resistant verticals and offering smaller “launch” packages.

Appendix idea: include 1–2 anonymized case studies with metrics (load time, conversion lift, lead quality).
